# BQ Guide for This Round

This is based on your prompt style and the expectations you wrote.

## Q1) "Tell me a time you had to learn something quickly."

What interviewer wants:

- structured learning process
- fast execution
- evidence of outcome

Good structure:

1. Situation
2. Learning plan (API docs, codebase walkthrough, shadowing, quick POC)
3. Execution timeline (day-by-day or week-by-week)
4. Result and metric

Template:

1. "I needed to deliver X in Y days on a stack I hadn't used."
2. "I created a 3-track learning plan: docs -> internal examples -> POC."
3. "I spent N hours/day, wrote a minimal working POC by day 2, validated with teammate."
4. "I shipped by date, and achieved metric M (latency, reliability, conversion, etc.)."

## Q2) "Tell me about disagreement with teammate/manager."

What interviewer wants:

- you can challenge constructively
- you use data/customer impact
- you accept valid feedback

Good structure:

1. State disagreement objectively.
2. Show evidence (data, user impact, risk).
3. Show collaboration behavior (not ego).
4. Show compromise and final win-win.

Template:

1. "We disagreed on A vs B approach."
2. "I suggested experiment with success criteria: p95 latency, error rate, user drop-off."
3. "Results showed approach X improved metric by Y%."
4. "I also accepted teammate's concern Z and combined both approaches."
5. "Final outcome improved both business and maintainability."

## Q3) "Tell me a challenging project."

Use STAR and quantify Action.

STAR:

- S: Business-critical context
- T: Your ownership
- A: Concrete steps and decisions
- R: Measured impact

Action examples with metrics:

- "Reduced p99 latency from 500ms to 180ms."
- "Cut incident rate by 35%."
- "Raised successful checkout rate by 12%."

## 60-second BQ answer skeleton

1. Context in one sentence.
2. Your responsibility.
3. 2-3 actions with rationale.
4. Hard metrics.
5. What you learned and reuse today.
